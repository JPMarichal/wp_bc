Emmeline B. Wells

Fifth General President of the Relief Society of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

Emmeline B. Wells was the fifth Relief Society General President for The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, serving between 1910 and 1921. Before becoming president, she was a secretary to prior general presidencies, an editor of the Latter-day Saint newspaper Woman’s Exponent, and an advocate for women’s rights and suffrage.

Emmeline Blanche Wells was born to David and Diadama Hare Woodward on February 29, 1828, in Petersham, Massachusetts. She joined the Church in 1842, when she was 14. Two years later, she moved to Nauvoo, Illinois, and in 1846, she left Nauvoo with other Latter-day Saints to make the trek to Utah.

Emmeline B. Wells became the senior editor and owner of the Woman’s Exponent in 1877. The newspaper was charged with publishing Latter-day Saint women’s experiences, literature, and political views. Her work with the newspaper and local organizations connected Utah women to the wider world. For decades, Emmeline B. Wells served on various committees of the National Woman Suffrage Association, the National and International Councils of Women, and the National Woman’s Press Association.

In 1912, she received an honorary degree from Brigham Young University. She remained a popular writer and orator as she served as Relief Society General President.

Emmeline B. Wells was married three times: to James Harvey Harris, to Newel K. Whitney as a plural wife, and to Daniel H. Wells as a plural wife. She had six children, three of whom preceded her in death. Emmeline B. Wells died on April 25, 1921.
