Lorenzo Snow

Fifth President of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

Lorenzo Snow was born April 3, 1814, in Mantua, Ohio. As a youth, he preferred academic study to an apprenticeship. His study of Hebrew and theology led to his conversion to the gospel of Jesus Christ in 1836. He spent the rest of his life in service as a missionary, Apostle, and Church President—a position he filled on September 13, 1898. He helped the Church recover from the challenges of the previous decades. He stabilized Church finances as members increased their tithes and offerings, and he expanded Church missionary efforts. As Church President at the dawning of the 20th century, he opened a new era in Latter-day Saint history. He died in Salt Lake City on October 10, 1901, at the age of 87.
