From The Encyclopedia of Mormonism

[From its beginning, The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints was frequently attacked and abused by the press. For a concise statement on the relationship of the press and the Church, see Press, News Media, and the Church. At the same time, the Church has used the printed word to convey its message to the world. For a fuller study of the Church's use of the printed word to produce books, pamphlets, broadsides, newspapers, and magazines, see Publications. For a listing of the periodicals and newspapers published by the Church, see Magazines; and Newspapers, LDS. For separate articles on several different publications see Almanacs; Bible: LDS Publication of the Bible; Bulletin; Conference Reports; Journal of Discourses; Juvenile Instructor; Liahona the Elders' Journal; Messenger and Advocate; Millennial Star; Nauvoo Neighbor; New Era; Relief Society Magazine; Times and Seasons; Utah Genealogical and Historical Magazine; Woman's Exponent; and Young Woman's Journal.]
